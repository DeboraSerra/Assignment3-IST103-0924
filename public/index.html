Debora's favorite hobby:
Spending time with Fred Weasley

My favorite hobby is spending time with Fred Weasley.

He is a half Shih Tzu, half Dachshund 4 year old male dog that came into my life to change it forever, and I love him for it!

The adoption

I like to joke that it was him who chose me, not the other way around.

Back then I wanted to adopt a cat, but a friend mentioned his dog had have some puppies and two were still looking for home. I decided to go and see the puppies, pretty much decided to adopt the one that was white with brown patches. Once I got there and tried to play with him, he got far away and layed down facing the other way. It couldn't be more clear, he didn't want anything to do with me.

On the other hand, the cute brown puppy came closer and started playing with my shoe. And when I tried to pick him up he came and even slept in my arms. That was the moment I decided to postpone adopting a cat a chose Fred to live with me.

At that moment he was still too young and couldn't leave his mom, so I waited and prepared until he was three months old so I could have him by my side!

Funniest stories

Little did I know that this cute puppy playing with my shoe was a bit of a monster! He had, and still has, so much energy and require a lot of play time to keep the house intact.

But when he first arrived, I didn't know that. And I had a job that required me to leave the house every day. With that, somehow he managed to destroy the plaster on the wall, the pipes on the sink, countless hygienic pads and every single toy he had.

One time I was so excited that I bought him this stuffed monkey that squeaked when he pressed it's arms and legs. He loved it! Carried it all around, like a little kid. The same day, when I came home from work, the monkey was in pieces. It was like a horror movie, with stuffing every where.

Another time, I noticed that the internet stopped working and when I went to reset the router, there he was... Chewing the router outlet cable. I was so scared that something would happen to him at that time, that I scared him too. Until today he doesn't go near cables.

Those a just a few of the many stories we had together for the past four years, and hopefully we will have many more in the years to come.

Curiosities about him

Fred is a very special dog. As people say that the dog always take after their tutor, he took after me and has many allergies, so I have to watch over him most of the time.

As unusual as it may sound, he is allergic to grass, chicken and cow protein, metal, dairy, and probably other things that I will find out eventually.

It took me a little over 3 years to discover this condition of his and realize that the health and skin problems he used to have constantly weren't my fault.

I believe that I only manage to find that out because I moved back to my parents (a place with a huge backyard where he plays on the grass every single day) when he was 2 years old and because of the Instagram algorithm.

Where he is now

The most difficult decision that I made in my life was leave him behind to come to Canada. Even though he is in a place that he knows and is used to, with people that love him.

The only reason I was able to make this decision is because I am sure this time apart won't be for long and because my mom promised to take care of him just like I did (including the weekly bath with the special shampoo, the sardines, the skin inspections to see if his allergies are worse and everything else).
